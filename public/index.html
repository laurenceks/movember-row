Why are we doing this?

Their Movember campaigns started in 2018 as a small group of Paramedics from Guildford who wanted to raise awareness for a great charity and have fun doing it. They took up the Movember’s signature challenge of growing a moustache for November. Since then, they’ve been fortunate enough to raise larger and larger amounts of money for this amazing charity each year, thanks in part to the addition of physical challenges. In 2018 they competed in Movember Make A Move, running 60km each for the 60 men lost to mental health every hour, raising over £5,000 as a result.

How does this work?

This is the fourth consecutive year the team have raised money for Movember. Each member of the team is passionate about the cause due to their experiences of men’s health issues in both their personal and professional lives.

In total they will row over 4,828km. Between working shifts as frontline paramedics, they will record rows and upload them to this website which will track the total distance rowed on a map across the Atlantic between the Canary Islands and Antigua.

Tell me more

Their Movember campaigns started in 2018 as a small group of Paramedics from Guildford who wanted to raise awareness for a great charity and have fun doing it. They took up the Movember’s signature challenge of growing a moustache for November. Since then, they’ve been fortunate enough to raise larger and larger amounts of money for this amazing charity each year, thanks in part to the addition of physical challenges. In 2018 they competed in Movember Make A Move, running 60km each for the 60 men lost to mental health every hour, raising over £5,000 as a result.

This year, they have set themselves an even bigger challenge: to row the Atlantic virtually, following the Tailisker Whiskey Atlantic Race Route from the Canary Islands to Antigua in the Caribbean - a distance of 4,828km.

With a small team of paramedics squeezing in rowing between shifts they hope to complete this by the end of November – which at over 480km per person will be no small feat! However, if they can’t complete it within November, then their Movember moustaches stay until they cross the ‘finish line’.

As paramedics they are sadly exposed to the devastating impact of male suicide too frequently, seeing the heart-breaking impact it has on families and loved ones. Each man lost to mental health is one too many.

Sadly this impact is felt not only with their patients but with their colleagues too. As many as 22% of ambulance service staff suffer with Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)2, a figure much higher than those actively serving in the armed forces (around 10-12%)3. A 2016 report by the UK mental health charity Mind found 91% of ambulance staff had reported poor mental health whilst in service4; further studies have found that 56% of ambulance staff report a level of compassion fatigue or burnout5. Clearly this is an issue that affects too many people, which is why they are so motivated to raise money to support the fantastic work Movember does in helping us to support men both at work and at home.

1 https://uk.movember.com/about/cause

2 Levels of mental health problems among UK emergency ambulance workers (Bennett et al. 2004)

3 Rescuers at risk: a systematic review and meta-regression analysis of the worldwide current prevalence and correlates of PTSD in rescue workers (Berger et al. 2018)
