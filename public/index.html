How does this work?

This year, for their seventh year raising money, the team will break into “escape pairs or teams” and race virtually from the famous POW camp in Poland to Scotts bar in London to complete their “home run”. In total, they will need to cover 1200km of distance; running, walking or cycling. That’s until they get to Calais and need to row or swim the distance of the channel. All this between working shifts as frontline Paramedics.

Tell me more

Their Movember campaigns started in 2018 as a small group of Paramedics from Guildford who wanted to raise awareness for a great charity and have fun doing it. They took up the Movember’s signature challenge of growing a moustache for November. Since then, they’ve been fortunate enough to raise larger and larger amounts of money for this amazing charity each year, thanks in part to the addition of physical challenges. In 2018 they competed in Movember Make A Move, running 60km each for the 60 men lost to mental health every hour, raising over £5,000 as a result. They built on this and in 2022 virtually rowed the Atlantic, covering 4720km as a team and raising a further £4,355. Now this year, they want to even bigger.

As paramedics they are sadly exposed to the devastating impact of male suicide too frequently, seeing the heart-breaking impact it has on families and loved ones. Each man lost to mental health is one too many.

Sadly this impact is felt not only with their patients but with their colleagues too. As many as 22% of ambulance service staff suffer with Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)2, a figure much higher than those actively serving in the armed forces (around 10-12%)3. A 2016 report by the UK mental health charity Mind found 91% of ambulance staff had reported poor mental health whilst in service4; further studies have found that 56% of ambulance staff report a level of compassion fatigue or burnout5. Clearly this is an issue that affects too many people, which is why they are so motivated to raise money to support the fantastic work Movember does in helping us to support men both at work and at home.
